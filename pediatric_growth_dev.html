⚠️ Developmental Red Flags — Infant
  • Unable to sit alone by 9 months
  • Unable to transfer objects hand to hand by 1 year
  • Abnormal pincer grip by 15 months
  • Unable to walk alone by 18 months
  • Failure to speak recognizable words by 2 years
⚠️ Enuresis / Bowel Control Milestones
  • Bowel control starts: 1 year, complete: 1.5 years
  • Daytime bladder control: 2–2.5 years
  • Night bladder control: 3.5–5 years

⚡ Neonatal Reflexes

Description, timing, and disappearance age — critical exam topic

Reflex Description Appears Disappears Type
Moro (Startle) ⭐ Sudden stimulus → arms fly out & up, tremble, slowly relax Birth ~2 months Protective
Tonic Neck ⭐ "Fencing" response: head turns → same-side arm/leg extend, opposite bend Birth 2–3 months Protective
Grasping ⭐ Neonate grasps any object firmly — enough to hold body weight Birth ~2 months Protective
Eye Blinking Eyelids close/open when stimulated by touch Birth Lifelong Protective
Crying Sudden pain, cold, hunger → air through vocal cords Birth Lifelong Protective
Sucking ⭐ Lips pucker, tongue rolls inward — caused by hunger/lip stimulation Birth 6–8 months (as reflex) Feeding
Rooting (Sucking) Touch of cheek/lips → head turns toward touch Birth 6 months Feeding
Swallowing Throat muscles close trachea, open esophagus when food in mouth Birth Lifelong Feeding
Gag Stimulation of uvula → reverse peristalsis, prevents entry of non-food Birth Lifelong Feeding
Respiratory Motion Chest & abdominal muscle contraction/relaxation → inspiration/expiration Birth Lifelong Breathing
Sneeze Violent reverse airflow from nose/throat Birth Lifelong Breathing
Cough Reverse airflow from throat and lungs 1 year Lifelong Breathing
Conditioned Reflexes Learned responses to various stimuli (e.g., stops crying when mother comes) 2 months Throughout life Conditioned
🧠 Reflex Disappearance Summary
@ ~2 months: Moro, Tonic Neck, Grasping disappear
@ 6 months: Rooting disappears
@ 6–8 months: Sucking (reflex) disappears
@ 1 year: Cough reflex appears (lifelong)
Lifelong from birth: Eye blink, Crying, Swallowing, Gag, Breathing, Sneeze

🧠 Memory Tricks & Shortcuts

Mnemonics, patterns and shortcuts to ace your exam

⚖️ Weight Rules (Most Tested!)
• Birth weight: 2.7–3.85 kg (avg ~3.25 kg)
• DOUBLES at 4–5 months
• TRIPLES at 12 months
• QUADRUPLES at 2 years (18-year-old doubles 6-year-old weight)

Infant gain rate:
0–4 mo = ¾ kg/mo · 5–8 mo = ½ kg/mo · 9–12 mo = ¼ kg/mo
Pattern: ¾ → ½ → ¼ (halving each trimester!)
📏 Toddler Formulas
Weight = (Age in years × 2) + 8
Height = (Age in years × 5) + 80

Example 2-yr-old: Weight = (2×2)+8 = 12 kg · Height = (2×5)+80 = 90 cm
🔲 Fontanel Closure (Common Exam Q)
Posterior Fontanel: closes at 2–6 months (early, triangular)
Anterior Fontanel: closes at 12–18 months (diamond-shaped)

Size of Anterior:
Birth = 3 fingers (4.5 cm) → 6 mo = 2 fingers (3 cm) → 12 mo = 1 finger (1.5 cm) → Closed at 12–18 mo
Pattern: 3→2→1→0 fingers!
🏃 Motor Milestones — "The 3-6-9-12 Rule"
3 months: Head control (holds head steady)
6 months: Sits briefly alone
9 months: Crawls (pull body in prone)
12 months: Walks alone!

Mnemonic: "Head, Sit, Crawl, Walk" — every 3 months!
⚡ Reflex Disappearance Trick
Moro, Tonic Neck, Grasp → all disappear at ~2 months
Remember: "MTG = 2 months Gone"

Sucking/Rooting → disappears at 6 months
Eye blink, Crying, Swallowing, Gag, Breathing, Sneeze = LIFELONG
💬 Language Milestones — "The Word Count"
9 mo: mama/dada · 10 mo: 1 word · 12 mo: 2+ words
15 mo: 4–6 words · 18 mo: 7–20 words
24 mo: ~50 words (2-word sentences)
2 yrs: 300 words → 5 yrs: 2100+ words

Pattern: words multiply rapidly — 50 → 300 → 2100!
💓 Vital Signs Trend
As child grows older:
Heart Rate ↓ (Neonate 120–150 → School Age 95)
Respiration ↓ (Neonate 35–50 → School Age 19–20)
Blood Pressure ↑ (Neonate 74/47 → School Age 100/60)
Temperature stays ≈ 37°C throughout
🦷 Teeth Tricks
First tooth: 6 months (central incisor, lower jaw first)
10 teeth at 1 year · 20 teeth at 2.5–3 years
Pattern: "Lower BEFORE Upper" for every tooth type
Cuspids & Molars: both at 16–20 months (easy to remember together)

→ TAII I → "Tiny Aunts In India Identify"
